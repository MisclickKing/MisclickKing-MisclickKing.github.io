Website Evaluations


Bob Saget

Lets get the bad out of the way first. This website is a self promoting site of the comedian named Bob Saget. It gives the viewer a discription of the man's works, links to his many socials, and a bunch of pictures of him standing in place making the exact same facial expression.

  • Contrast: The contrast varies for the most part. Most of the text is white set against a mostly dark grey background, which works well. However there are areas where the same white text is placed in front of a white or light blue background, which makes you squint to read it.
  • Repetition: The site repeats the same color schemes of grey and white, as well as large rectangular shapes for images and text layout. However the size of these rectangles and the spacing of them (or lack thereof) create a very tight and uncomfortable feeling when trying to navigate the site. Not to mention a lot of 'white space' is created near the top and bottom of the site because of this.
  • Alignment: Nothing is truly aligned properly on this site. Pictures and lumps of text feel thrown together without a care as to how it relates to or effects one another.
  • Proximity: The site is broken up into three main pieces excluding the navigation area at the top. However, the content barely as room to breath as these parts are so close to one another. Even within these three sections, the text and pictures are either too close together or generated too much 'white space' to justify the formatting choice.

Chiara Luzzana

Now for the good. This site is another self promoting site of a musical artist named Chiara Luzzana. The site as a whole is minimal, easy to navigate, and is accompanied by a wonderful looping track. The site links to her works, socails, and introduction.

  • Contrast: The site has a beautiful two tone contrast. It uses what I believe is a cream color with a hint of pink, and a very dark brown being closer to black. Transitions to differnent elements of the site will change the dominate presence of the two colors (e.g. dark brown background with cream color text and vise versa).
  • Repetition: The site has a lot of repeating shapes fonts styles. Rectangles and squares are used for pictures and navigation in certain areas. Larger text has a distint font compared to the smaller text, which makes it easier to naviagate the site and its more specific elements.
  • Alignment: Everything aligns well, keeping text and images to certain parts of the screen where they are relevant and easily displayed. The site even plays with this alignment and encourages you to line up certain pictures while you scroll through.
  • Proximity: Every element of this site is spaced out far enough from each other that it is easy to find and read the text where relevant, while being close enough to where nothing feels out of place either.
